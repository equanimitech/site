## Empirical anchors for future writing

- Desbordes 2015 (Mindfulness journal): "Moving Beyond Mindfulness, Defining Equanimity as an Outcome Measure." Key paper defining equanimity scientifically.
- Taylor 2011: experienced Zen meditators perceive emotional stimuli as less intense without PFC top-down control or amygdala down-regulation. Equanimity is NOT cognitive reappraisal.
- Desbordes 2012: 8 weeks of mindful attention training produced longitudinal decrease in right amygdala activation, measured at rest. Trait-level change.
- Hadash et al. 2016: Decoupling Model. Equanimity = "the decoupling of desire (wanting and not wanting) from the hedonic tone of current or anticipated experience." This is the formal scholarly version of the lab's thesis.
- 2024 meta-analysis of 19 RCTs: mindfulness-based interventions on problematic internet use, effect size g = -1.67. Very large.
- Grayscale screens: 22 to 57 minute reduction in daily screen time.
- AER 2022 study: causal negative impact of Facebook on student mental health (staggered college rollout natural experiment).
- JMIR Mental Health 2022 meta-analysis: problematic social media use correlates with depression r=0.273, anxiety r=0.348, stress r=0.313.

## McMindfulness positioning

- Purser 2019: $125M US military mindfulness spend as "stripped from any sort of ethical or moral context."
- Headspace, Calm use streaks, rewards, gamification: engagement mechanics at odds with their stated purpose.
- UW review of 370 mindfulness apps: "mindfulness is presented primarily as a tool for relaxation and stress reduction." Teachers concerned it neglects full potential.
- "Designing for mindfulness vs designing for equanimity is paradigmatic, not incremental."

## Comparative traditions (for an essay, not the landing)

- Taoism wu-wei: parallel to equanimity, but metaphysical-doctrinal vs methodical. Frontier farmer parable ("who knows if this is good or bad?").
- Stoic apatheia: rational judgment vs non-reactive observation. Preserves rational agent; Buddhism dissolves the agent (anattā).
- Bhagavad Gita sthitaprajña: similar outward form, grounded in eternal Atman.
- Sufi rida: contentment with divine will. Theistic and relational.
- What's unique to Buddhist equanimity: three marks of existence + Brahmavihara safeguard (loving-kindness, compassion, joy prevent collapse into indifference).

## The three pillars (good summary for a primer intro)

Equanimity as engagement's successor rests on:
1. Neurologically distinct from emotional regulation (no PFC top-down required).
2. Structurally resistant to McMindfulness because it requires companion qualities (mettā, karuṇā, muditā).
3. Measurable via validated constructs (EQUA-S, ES-16, Decoupling Model).
